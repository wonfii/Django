Leonardo da Vinci

About Leonardo

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance period, celebrated for his contributions to art, science, and engineering. He is widely regarded as one of the most diversely talented individuals ever to have lived.

Famous Works

Leonardo created some of the most iconic artworks in history, including:

  • Mona Lisa - Known for her enigmatic smile.
  • The Last Supper - A masterpiece depicting the moment Jesus announces his betrayal.
  • Vitruvian Man - A famous sketch exploring human proportions.

Scientific Achievements

Beyond art, Leonardo made groundbreaking contributions to anatomy, engineering, and physics. His notebooks contain designs for flying machines, military weapons, and studies of human anatomy that were centuries ahead of their time.
